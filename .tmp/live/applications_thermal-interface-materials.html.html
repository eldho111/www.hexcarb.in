Application Pathway

Thermal Interface Materials

For cooling-limited systems where interface resistance drives reliability and performance risk. This pathway maps package geometry, pressure limits, and heat flux conditions to HexTherm solutions.

Why this pathway

TIM performance depends on both bulk thermal resistance and interface contact resistance. Reliable outcomes require matching bond-line thickness, compressibility, and clamping pressure to real operating cycles.

  • Reduce contact resistance between package and sink while preserving mechanical stability.
  • Match compressibility and thickness to tolerance stack and mounting pressure.
  • Validate thermal cycling stability before production transition.

Material pathways

  1. Film route

    Thin thermal films for constrained gaps and controlled bond-line thickness in EMI-sensitive architectures.

  2. Pad route

    Compressible pad formats for assembly tolerance absorption and stable interface contact.

  3. High-load route

    Higher heat-load pathways for dense compute and power-electronics use-cases.
