Application Pathway

Different Types of Dispersions

For programs where rheology, agglomeration control, and process compatibility determine coating quality and electrical consistency. This pathway maps coating and slurry requirements to HexFlow and HexSheets dispersion families.

Why this pathway

Dispersion quality strongly influences conductivity repeatability, film uniformity, and manufacturability. Solvent/dispersant choice and shear history should be matched to CNT and graphene geometry to limit aggregation during scale-up.

  • Match solvent polarity and binder chemistry to your substrate and process window.
  • Balance conductivity target with viscosity and print/coating constraints.
  • Qualify storage and shear stability before pilot deployment.

Material pathways

  1. Aqueous route

    Use for water-compatible process lines and lower-VOC workflows. Start with HexFlow A-Series.

  2. Solvent route

    Use when resin compatibility or drying profile requires solvent systems. Start with HexFlow S-Series.

  3. Graphene dispersion route

    Use for barrier and printable coating programs where platelet orientation and film continuity matter.
