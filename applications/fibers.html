Application Pathway

Fibers

For teams building conductive, sensing, or reinforced flexible structures. This pathway maps textile architecture and integration constraints to HexWeave formats.

Why this pathway

Fiber-based nanocarbon systems can support lightweight conductivity and embedded sensing, but final performance depends on yarn architecture, contact design, and cyclic durability.

  • Balance conductivity targets against bend radius, strain range, and fatigue cycles.
  • Select yarn architecture for weaving, lamination, or surface integration.
  • Plan connector, terminal, and environmental test strategy early in prototype stages.

Material pathways

  1. Sensing yarn route

    For strain monitoring and responsive textile behavior where repeatability over flex cycles is critical.

  2. Conductive fiber route

    For low-mass current pathways where metal alternatives can add weight or stiffness.

  3. Tape route

    For directional reinforcement and guided conductive runs across laminate or composite stacks.
