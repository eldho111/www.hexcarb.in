Application Pathway

SWCNT and Graphene Hybrid Materials

For programs needing balanced electrical, thermal, and structural outcomes. This pathway combines nanotube and graphene families for multi-property optimization where ratio control and dispersion quality are critical across composites, inks, and masterbatch systems.

Why this pathway

Hybrid systems can provide complementary behavior versus single-filler routes when ratios and interfaces are engineered correctly. This approach can support electrical network continuity, crack-arrest behavior, and improved heat spreading within one formulation strategy.

  • Use SWCNT for network continuity and graphene for planar transport and reinforcement effects.
  • Tune filler ratio and dispersion route for the target process window and property priorities.
  • Validate interfacial compatibility and agglomeration control before scaling to larger batches.

Material pathways

  1. Lab exploration route

    Start with HexTubes R&D powders and HexSheets powders for ratio screening and formulation mapping.

  2. Pilot production route

    Move to industrial nanotube grades and hybrid masterbatch systems after early validation.

  3. Composite integration route

    Use HexBlend systems to reduce process complexity in target resin platforms.
