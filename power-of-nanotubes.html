Strength

Load both beams until one gives up

The hexagonal carbon bond is the strongest link chemistry offers. Pull the handle down: the steel truss deforms, then fails. The honeycomb flexes — and comes back.

100 GPa tensile strength — ~50–100× structural steel

Electrical

Race electrons down both wires

In copper, electrons scatter off lattice defects every ~40 nanometers. In a nanotube they travel ballistically — no scattering for microns at a time. Hold the panel and watch the difference.

1000× maximum current density of copper (~10⁹ A/cm²)
Thermal

Touch it and watch heat travel

Move your pointer across the network. Heat travels as lattice vibrations — phonons — and the stiff carbon lattice carries them almost without loss. The polymer strip below barely notices the same touch.

3500 W/m·K thermal conductivity — about 9× copper

Flexibility

Bend it past where steel breaks

Bend the tube to extreme curvature and release. It springs back, perfectly elastic. A steel rod of the same proportion kinks long before that — and stays kinked.
