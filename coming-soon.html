We are preparing the public release of the HexCarb AI Engine. The goal is practical acceleration: faster material screening, better experiment planning, and cleaner transition from R&D trials to procurement-ready qualification.

Why This Matters

Why AI is now critical for faster advanced-carbon R&D

CNT, graphene, GO/rGO, and hybrid systems sit in a high-dimensional design space: precursor quality, dispersion route, loading window, interface chemistry, process temperature, and reliability constraints all interact. Traditional trial-and-error can consume months before a viable process window appears.

Search Space Compression

AI helps rank candidate experiments so teams test better conditions earlier instead of scanning blindly.

Closed-Loop Learning

Each experiment updates the model, so optimization quality improves as data accumulates.

Faster Qualification

Structured data trails make it easier to move from lab insight to supplier-ready and customer-facing evidence.

Dedicated AI Advantage

Why companies will need dedicated AI, not only generic assistants

Generic models are useful for drafting and search, but materials decisions require domain-specific data, quality controls, and process context that public models do not reliably capture on their own.

Private Process Memory

Dedicated systems learn from internal lots, pilot outcomes, and customer constraints without exposing proprietary know-how.

Materials-Specific Reasoning

The model can be tuned to carbon morphology, purity, dispersion behavior, and qualification rules specific to your stack.

Standards-Aligned Language

Terminology and evidence labels can be enforced against accepted standards for cleaner cross-team communication.

Decision Traceability

Every recommendation can be tied to source experiments, methods, and confidence states for engineering and procurement review.

Autonomous Materials Lab
