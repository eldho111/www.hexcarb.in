    Materials Evolution Map

    How major carbon nanomaterial families evolved in parallel

    Each family matured through different synthesis, characterization, and reliability constraints, then converged in modern hybrid applications.

    SWCNT

    High-aspect-ratio conductive pathways used in sensing, flexible conductors, and lightweight performance composites.

    MWCNT

    Reinforcement and conductivity-focused systems used in polymer compounding and EMI/ESD engineering workflows.

    Graphene + GO/rGO

    Planar transport and barrier architectures for coatings, thermal systems, inks, and multifunctional composite design.

    Fullerenes

    Molecular carbon allotropes that shaped nanoscale carbon chemistry and niche electronics research pathways.

    Nanodiamond

    Hardness and surface chemistry-oriented particles used in polishing, tribology, coatings, and specialty formulations.

    Nanostructured Carbon Black

    Conductive carbon-black systems are used in industrial compounds for ESD, EMI, and process-stable conductivity tuning.

    CNF + Hybrid Carbon Systems

    Bridging architectures that combine conductivity, processability, and reinforcement across industrial material stacks.
