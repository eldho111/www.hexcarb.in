    Material Families

    Seven families of carbon nanomaterials

    Each family took a different path from the lab to industry. Here's where they stand today.

    Single-Wall Nanotubes

    Extremely thin, highly conductive tubes used in sensors, flexible electronics, and high-performance composites.

    Multi-Wall Nanotubes

    Stronger, easier to produce. Used to add conductivity and strength to plastics, coatings, and shielding materials.

    Graphene & Graphene Oxide

    Flat, atom-thin sheets with exceptional thermal and electrical properties. Used in coatings, inks, thermal pads, and composites.

    Fullerenes

    Tiny carbon cages (C60) that opened up molecular-scale carbon science. Still used in electronics and specialty chemistry.

    Nanodiamond

    Ultra-hard nanoparticles used in polishing, wear-resistant coatings, and surface treatments.

    Conductive Carbon Black

    Nanostructured carbon particles that add electrical conductivity to plastics, rubber, and industrial compounds.

    Carbon Nanofibers & Hybrids

    Combine the best properties of multiple carbon forms — conductivity, strength, and processability — in one system.
